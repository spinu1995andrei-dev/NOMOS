 # NOMOS: Starting Point

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** open concept / public draft  
**Purpose:** free development, critique, improvement, forking, implementation  
**Authorship:** the idea does not belong to one person

---

## 1. Foreword

NOMOS is an idea for a new digital infrastructure of trust, value, and free exchange.

It is not a company.  
It is not an investment product.  
It is not a promise of profit.  
It is not a political organization.  
It does not belong to an author, team, foundation, or government.

NOMOS is an attempt to formulate a protocol where value can exist without dependence on arbitrariness, inflation, censorship, intermediaries, or hidden power.

The main message is simple:

> **A person’s value must belong to the person.  
> The rules of money must be open.  
> Individual privacy must be the norm.  
> No one should hold power over the system.**

This document is a starting point.

It is created for anyone who wishes to argue, improve, design, code, audit, fork, document, build wallets, nodes, apps, and alternative implementations.

---

## 2. NOMOS Manifest

The modern world is built on systems of trust that often require obedience to intermediaries.

Banks can freeze your account.  
Payment systems can refuse access.  
Governments can devalue currencies.  
Corporations can control infrastructure.  
Platforms can exclude people from economic life.  
Even digital assets often depend on foundations, teams, exchanges, influencers, and centralized points of failure.

NOMOS offers another principle:

> **Don’t ask for trust – replace it with open rules.**

We believe everyone should have the right to:

- store value without permission;
- transfer value without an intermediary;
- protect their financial privacy;
- disclose information only voluntarily;
- participate in the network regardless of country, status, background, or capital;
- verify the system’s rules independently;
- use money that cannot be secretly printed;
- access the economy without discrimination.

NOMOS is not created against anyone.  
It is created **for people**.

NOMOS should not be a weapon against society.  
It must be the foundation of an honest digital society.

---

## 3. Main Goal

The goal of NOMOS is to create an open digital protocol of value that combines:

1. Limited and transparent emission.
2. Default user privacy.
3. Absence of owners or central administrators.
4. Accessibility for ordinary people, not just big players.
5. Protection from censorship, arbitrary freezing, and hidden control.
6. Ability to build services, markets, apps, and public tools on top.
7. Ethical principle: privacy for citizens, transparency for public power.

---

## 4. Main Principle

NOMOS is built around a single main principle:

> **No one should have unilateral power over another’s value.**

This means:

- no one can create coins outside the rules;
- no one can block another’s wallet;
- no one can view another’s transactions without consent;
- no one can change the rules for their own benefit;
- no one can be the “official owner” of the protocol;
- no one can forcibly rewrite the network history;
- no one can make themselves an exception to the rules.

---

## 5. The NOMOS Constitution

### 5.1. Open Emission Rules

No unit of value can be created outside of pre-published and verifiable rules.

No hidden premine.  
No secret reserves.  
No privileged addresses.  
No “founders’ coins” bypassing the community.

---

### 5.2. No Owner

NOMOS must have no owner.

There may be an initiator of the idea.  
There may be developers.  
There may be teams.  
There may be forks.  
There may be different implementations.

But there must not be a subject who can say:

> “This belongs to me, and I decide for everyone.”

---

### 5.3. Default Privacy

Financial privacy is not a crime.  
It is a basic human right.

NOMOS should hide:

- sender;
- receiver;
- amount;
- links between transactions;
- user’s financial history.

Disclosure is possible only voluntarily – through mechanisms like view-keys or zero-knowledge proofs.

---

### 5.4. Voluntary Transparency

A user can prove payment, balance, audit income or source only by their own decision.

Public organizations, funds, cities, governments, and social projects may use NOMOS for transparent treasuries if they choose transparent mode.

Principle:

> **A citizen has the right to privacy.  
> Public power should strive for transparency.**

---

### 5.5. No Arbitrary Confiscation

The protocol must not contain functions for external freezing, confiscation, or administrative seizure of funds.

If such functions appear, it is no longer NOMOS but another project.

---

### 5.6. Full Node Accessibility

Ordinary people must be able to participate in the network.

A full node should not require a datacenter, huge capital, or high-end equipment.

NOMOS should strive for regular computers, small servers, and distributed devices to be able to run the network.

---

### 5.7. Protection from Centralization

The system should be designed to discourage absolute dominance by large players.

If participating is profitable only for the rich, the network will become a new financial aristocracy.

NOMOS must seek a balance between security, economic incentives, and accessibility for ordinary users.

---

### 5.8. Security Above Speed

Speed matters.  
Convenience matters.  
Scalability matters.

But security, verifiability, and independence matter more.

Better a slow honest system than a fast one with invisible internal power.

---

### 5.9. Simplicity of the Core

The basic protocol must be as simple, formally verifiable, and robust as possible.

Complex apps, markets, computations, and services should be on higher layers, not overload the core.

---

### 5.10. Free Forking

Anyone or any group has the right to fork the code, documents, or ideas and propose their own implementation.

A fork is not betrayal.  
A fork is the natural mechanism of evolution for open systems.

---

## 6. Economic Idea

NOMOS can use two categories of digital units.

---

### 6.1. NOM

**NOM** – the core scarce value unit of the network.

Expected properties:

- limited or predictable emission;
- no premine;
- open supply rules;
- used for security, fees, collateral, settlement, and long-term value storage.

---

### 6.2. Stable Settlement Units

People need stable accounting for daily life.

On top of NOMOS there may exist stable units, such as:

- nUSD;
- nEUR;
- nLOCAL;
- regional or commodity units;
- settlement tokens with various forms of backing.

Importantly: stable units must not undermine the independence of the base protocol.

---

## 7. Fair Launch Principle

NOMOS must not start with privilege.

Desired launch conditions:

- no ICO;
- no private sale;
- no premine;
- no hidden investors;
- no founders’ addresses in genesis;
- open-source code before launch;
- public specification;
- anyone can run a node from day one;
- no “official company” controlling the launch.

If the launch is not fair, the network will carry this stain forever.

## 8. Technical Direction

This document is not a final technical specification.  
It sets the direction.

NOMOS should strive for the following architecture:

---

### 8.1. Layer 1: The Core

The core must provide:

- issuance and accounting of NOM;
- transaction validation;
- double-spending protection;
- consensus;
- privacy;
- basic fees;
- censorship resistance;
- verifiable history;
- minimal and secure logic.

---

### 8.2. Layer 2: Fast Payments & Apps

Second layer can provide:

- fast micropayments;
- payment channels;
- inter-app settlements;
- subscriptions;
- escrow;
- markets;
- business integrations;
- machine payments;
- AI and IoT services.

---

### 8.3. Layer 3: Markets & Public Systems

On top of the network there may be:

- storage market;
- computation market;
- microtask market;
- crowdfunding;
- insurance;
- transparent charity funds;
- public budgets;
- science foundations;
- decentralized services for cities, communities, and DAOs.

---

## 9. Privacy

NOMOS should research and apply modern cryptographic privacy, such as:

- zero-knowledge proofs;
- stealth addresses;
- one-time addresses;
- hidden amounts;
- voluntary audit mechanisms;
- metadata protection;
- potential post-quantum resilience.

Privacy must not be an addon – it must be the standard.

If only some use privacy, they are singled out.  
If everyone uses privacy, everyone is protected.

---

## 10. Post-Quantum Direction

NOMOS must consider a future where quantum computing might endanger current cryptography.

This does not mean using unproven solutions in haste.

It means:

- monitor post-quantum standards;
- design for key migration;
- avoid un-upgradable architecture;
- conduct open research;
- not promise absolute invulnerability;
- build a system able to adapt.

---

## 11. Consensus

The final consensus mechanism must be subject to separate research.

Possible directions:

- Proof of Work;
- Proof of Stake;
- hybrid models;
- Proof of Useful Work;
- Proof of Service;
- Proof of Storage;
- reputation constraints without centralized identity;
- anti-whale/anti-cluster mechanisms.

Selection criteria:

1. security;
2. decentralization;
3. verifiability;
4. censorship resistance;
5. accessibility for regular people;
6. long-term sustainability;
7. no dependence on a single group.

---

## 12. What NOMOS Must Not Become

NOMOS must **not**:

- promise people riches;
- rely on personality cults;
- depend on one leader;
- sell itself with hype;
- become a pyramid scheme;
- use closed source code;
- have hidden admin keys;
- support administrative freezing of funds;
- depend on a single company;
- proclaim one website/repo as “the only real one”;
- become a tool of fraud, oppression, or control.

---

## 13. Ethical Position

NOMOS should serve freedom, not chaos.  
Privacy, not irresponsibility.  
Independence, not the destruction of society.  
Technology, not tech-worship.

The main ethical formula:

> **Freedom for individuals must be combined with impossibility of hidden power over them.**

NOMOS recognizes:

- the right to economic autonomy;
- the right of society to transparent public institutions;
- the right of developers to free labor;
- the right of users to verifiable rules;
- the right of future generations not to depend on today’s system mistakes.
